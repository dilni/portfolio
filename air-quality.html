Self Project Data Science & Public Health

Air Quality, Weather & Respiratory Health Analysis

A multi-domain data science project investigating the direct public health impacts of environmental factors using predictive modeling.

1 / 11

Project Overview

The system ingests disparate atmospheric metrics, cross-references them with municipal clinical admission logs, cleans anomalies, and uses correlation analytics alongside linear regression modeling to map how pollution fluctuations drive hospital respiratory admission spikes.

1. Data Fusion & Exploratory Analytics Engine

Processes unorganized environmental and medical records into a singular, highly integrated analysis pipeline.

Data Aggregation Pipeline

Imports, cleans, and merges multi-source historical datasets (PM2.5 levels, temperature variances, humidity levels, and hospital intake counts) using Pandas.

Statistical Correlation Arrays

Executes exploratory data analysis (EDA) to map statistical correlations between ambient air quality indexes and respiratory infection rates.

Data Visualization Studio

Renders complex scatter plots, trend lines, and heatmaps using Matplotlib to expose hidden environmental patterns clearly.

2. Predictive Health Modeling & Reporting

Applies machine learning to provide actionable forewarning metrics for public health infrastructure.

Linear Regression Modeler

Establishes a predictive model mapping environmental predictors directly to continuous hospital admission targets.

Feature Weight Evaluations

Computes regression coefficient values to rank which environmental factors (e.g., particulate matter vs. sudden temperature drops) present the highest medical risk profiles.

Reporting Utilities

Automatically compiles derived insights and visualization assets into analytical summaries to support data-driven public health strategy formulations.
